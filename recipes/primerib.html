Prime Rib

Prime ribs have always been a staple dish for the holidays. I much prefer it over turkey. I've found that the best method I've used was the reverse sear method popularized by Food Lab

Ingredients

  • Prime Rib, preferably good quality or dry aged
  • Salt
  • Pepper

Steps

  1. Season prime rib with salt and pepper at least the day before (up to 4 days before)
  2. Preheat oven to 150 degrees F and place prime rib in oven for 6 hours (or 250 degrees for 3 hours)
  3. When prime rib reaches internal temperature of 125-130, remove from oven and cover with foil for 30 minutes up to an hour and half
  4. When ready to serve, turn up oven to 500-550 degrees F and place prime rib in oven for 5-15 minutes
